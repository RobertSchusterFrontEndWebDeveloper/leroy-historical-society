history

Established in 1973, the LeRoy Area Historical Society has been preserving the history of LeRoy. The current museum was donated to the historical society in 1986 by area residents Harold and Alberta Nelson. They donated the 1901 building that was the former LeRoy Exchange Bank on Bevins Street.

fundraisers

The LeRoy Area Historical Society uses fundraisers to aid in covering the costs for collecting and cataloging items deemed to be of historical significance to the LeRoy area. Cemetery and family genealogies are also cataloged with this financial help.

hours

At this time you can visit the museum by appointment.
Visits can be scheduled through members of the LeRoy Area Historical Society. We encourage visitors to the area and groups, such as schools, to contact us for a special view our collection.

about us

At a special dedication, during the July 11, 1987 Razzasque Days festival, the former LeRoy Exchange Bank building was donated to the LeRoy Area Historical Society to become the LeRoy Area Museum. The museum is maintained and supported by the LeRoy Area Historical Society which was assembled for the 1973 LeRoy village centennial. Area residents have collected, and continue to collect, artifacts and records that have a significant relationship to the LeRoy area.
